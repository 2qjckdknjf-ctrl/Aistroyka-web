# Market Expansion Strategy

**Phase 10 — Market Expansion & Revenue Scaling (MAX)**  
**Multi-region, multi-industry scaling without core domain rewrites.**

---

## Target regions prioritization

| Priority | Region | Rationale | Entry timing |
|----------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **1** | Home (RU/CIS) | Default locale (ru); existing pilot and early adopters; regulatory familiarity. | First (current) |
| **2** | EU (single market) | Large construction and field-services market; GDPR-aligned; English + local languages. | Phase 2 |
| **3** | North America (US/CA) | High SaaS adoption; English primary; construction and facilities. | Phase 2–3 |
| **4** | LATAM (e.g. MX, BR, ES-speaking) | Spanish/Portuguese already in roadmap (es); growing construction; price-sensitive. | Phase 3 |
| **5** | APAC (e.g. AU, SEA) | English; different labor and compliance norms; evaluate after EU/NA. | Phase 4 |

**Criteria:** Demand (construction/field-services density), language fit (existing or planned locales), regulatory clarity, payment infrastructure, and partner interest.

---

## Market entry sequencing

1. **Consolidate home region:** Prove product-market fit and pilot-to-paid conversion in primary market (RU/CIS or initial region). Document case studies and reference customers.
2. **EU entry:** Add/enhance locales (e.g. de, fr); ensure GDPR and data-residency; land 2–3 design partners or pilots; then general availability.
3. **NA entry:** English-first; Stripe/payment and legal (e.g. US terms); partner or direct sales; then self-serve or hybrid.
4. **LATAM:** Spanish (es) and Portuguese (pt) if added; local payment (e.g. Mercado Pago) and pricing; partner-led where possible.
5. **APAC:** After EU/NA; assess localization and compliance; partner or distributor model.

**Rule:** One region at a time for launch; reuse platform (no regional forks). Localize UI, pricing, and go-to-market only.

## Competitive landscape summary

- **Field management / task apps:** Generic task tools (Asana, Monday) lack field-report and photo workflow; construction-specific tools (Procore, Buildertrend, PlanGrid) are heavy and often enterprise-only. **Gap:** Lightweight assign–report–review with AI and mobile-first.
- **Report/photo tools:** Standalone photo or form apps don’t tie to tasks and projects; spreadsheets and email are fragmented. **Gap:** End-to-end task → report → review with visibility and audit.
- **AI for construction:** Emerging; often bolt-on to existing tools. **Gap:** Built-in AI for report analysis and insights without separate vendor.
- **Positioning:** “Field visibility and report workflow for construction and field teams—assign tasks, get photo reports, review in one place, with AI when you need it.”

---

## Positioning strategy

- **Who:** Small to mid-size construction and field-services teams (general contractors, subcontractors, renovation, development).
- **What:** Task assignment, field reports with photos, manager review, sync and notifications, optional AI analysis—all in one platform (web + mobile).
- **Why:** Replace spreadsheets and WhatsApp; one source of truth; faster review; full audit trail; optional AI to spot issues and summarize.
- **How:** SaaS; Free/Pro/Enterprise; invite-based teams; no heavy implementation. Industry solution packs (templates, tasks, KPIs) speed time-to-value.

**Tagline (example):** “Assign. Report. Review. One platform for field and office.”

---

## Differentiation pillars

1. **Unified loop:** Assign → do → report → review in one product; not a patchwork of task + form + storage.
2. **Mobile-first workers:** Native iOS/Android Worker apps; offline queue and sync; built for field conditions.
3. **Manager and worker in one ecosystem:** Same tenant; roles (owner/admin/member/viewer); no separate “contractor app” vs “client app” fragmentation.
4. **AI built in:** Image analysis and summaries tied to reports; governance and safety controls; no separate AI vendor.
5. **Industry-ready:** Solution packs (templates, tasks, KPIs) by vertical (residential, commercial, GC, renovation, development) to shorten onboarding.
6. **Enterprise-ready:** RBAC, audit, retention, multi-tenant scaling, and compliance posture (Phase 7–9) without custom build.

---

## Execution

- **Reuse platform:** No core domain rewrites; expand via locales, industry packs, pricing, and GTM.
- **Metrics:** Track pipeline and revenue by region and industry; see EXPANSION_ANALYTICS.
- **Review:** Quarterly update of region priority and entry timing based on pipeline and capacity.
